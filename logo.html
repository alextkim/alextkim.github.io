About this Project

As an active member of CCM (Crossroads Campus Ministries) I had the opportunity to enter CCM's annual sweater design competition. I had started to use Illustrator and Photoshop about a month before they announced the design competition so I thought it would be a great first project to test out my technical skills. It turned out to be a pretty fun project that I learned a lot from and got to help out my college ministry.


The Client

my friends!

I had gotten to know many of the members personally throughout the years and realized that even though they were all unique as individuals they shared many similar traits. When designing these logos I had a few keywords kept in mind:

    1. Christian
    2. Hipster
    3. College student
    4. Asian-American
    5. Quirky

My Process

With these things in mind I made it my goal to make a logo that best represented the atmosphere of CCM. I started off with a lot of hand-sketches (mostly doodles in class) to brainstorm and implemented my ideas using Illustrator. I mostly stuck with black and white logos for this project because I figured that color could be added later depending on the sweater’s base color.



The Result

Sweaters!

In the end, the first logo I made ended up being chosen. The first logo wasn’t my favorite but like everyone else, I only had one vote. Although it was not an easy experience making all these logos only to see one that I didn’t really like get picked, I learned that not everyone has the same vision as me and that it’s important to have everyone’s input. This experience was also good practice to take my pride out of my own work and receive open criticism.
