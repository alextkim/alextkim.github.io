Featured Work: "CCM Logos"

Original size: n/a

Purpose: Logo for CCM's Annual Sweater

Tools: Adobe Illustrator

Why I started this project

As part of an active member of Crossroads Campus Ministries (CCM) I was able to participate in the annual sweater design competition. Each year CCM decides on their favorite logo submitted by members. CCM was and still is a very small ministry composing of around 20 active attendees so for the last year I competed I was the only one to submit any logos. Knowing this I decided to make a bunch of logos and submit them all for critique. In the end I made around 6 different logos but chose the 3 above to showcase because they were my favorites.

The Target Audience

I was very fortunate for this project since the people reviewing my work and using my work were the same. I had gotten to know many of the members personally throughout the years and realized that even though they were all different as individuals they shared many similar traits. When designing these logos I had a few keywords kept in mind:

1. Christian

2. Hipster

3. College student

4. Asian-American

5. Quirky

The Process

With these things in mind I made it my goal to make a logo that best represented the atmosphere of CCM. For this project I primarily used hand-sketches and Adobe illustrator to brainstorm and implement my ideas. I mostly stuck with black and white logos for this project because I figured that color could be added later depending on the sweater’s base color.


The Result

In the end, the first logo I made ended up being chosen. The first logo wasn’t my favorite but like everyone else, I only had one vote. Although it was not an easy experience making all these logos only to see one that I didn’t really like get picked, I learned that not everyone has the same vision as me and that it’s important to have everyone’s input. This experience was also good practice to take my pride out of my own work and receive open criticism.
