Project: House Manager

Roles: lead, designer, illustrator

Type: physical product


What is a House Manager?

UCSD's Price Center is the campus center for events, food, and clubs. The crew behind these events is the House Manager department. As a house manager myself, I saw a lot of opportunity for improvement within the department and implemented design-thinking to help my work environment.

The House Manager department sets up events in the rooms and ballrooms in Price Center for various organizations





The Problem Space

  • Lack of Communication
  • Incomplete Setups
  • Fine Details being missed

    • My team and wanted to target these specific problems in the House Manager Department in hopes of creating a more efficient team dynamic and improving our department's reputation.




    Collecting Data

    1. Observations

  • Multiple viewpoints (noob, lead, etc)
  • Looked at the daily HM shifts
  • Tracked the pitfalls at work
  • Observed during shifts (live)

    • 2. Interviews

  • 20+ interviews
  • Good qualitative data
  • Got to hear what other workers deal with personally

    • 3. Suggestion box

  • Initial form of data collection
  • Quantitative + qualitative
  • Downside: Mostly rants

    • 4. Hangouts

  • Hanging outside the workplace
  • Getting to know HMs personally
  • Getting to workers on a behavioral level






    • Putting It Together

    After gathering some intial data, we came together as a team to consolidate the data that we had to find patterns. Using methods like affinity diagrams and priority charts we had a lot of great discussions.





    Personas

    The anchor points we created from getting know our users to remind us of them. We wanted our finished product to be used easily by all of these catagories but we especially emphasized the "noob" category because we noticed that they were a demographic that had the most confusion.

    The Noob

  • Less than 3 months of work
  • Usually clueless
  • Needs explicit instruction

    • The Experienced

  • 3 -11 months of work
  • Understands the rules and regulations
  • Lacks initiative but follows orders

    • The lead

  • 1 year+ of work
  • Takes initiative and responsibility
  • Communicates with prior and next shifts regularly

    • Our Boss

  • Gives verbal instructions to HMs
  • Checks on the work done by HMs
  • Heavily invested in the job



    • Concepts

    After getting to know the environment and workers, we moved on to a phase of rapid ideation to generate lots of possible solutions.








    Prototypes

    After talking over our concepts we decided to see some of them come to life by creating prototype versions of them. These were helpful because we were able to get feedback from our users because now users could tangibly interact with our ideas (even though some of them were a bit sloppy looking lol).

    1. The Trifold clipBoard

    Our very first prototype! House Managers (HMs) already had a system in place that involved carrying a clipboard to keep track of work, so we tried to improve upon that existing solution by finding the pitfalls and coming up with a new design.

    2. The Bifold clipBoard

    After getting some feedback on the Trifold prototype we decided to tweak our design a bit. The trifold was not very intuitive for users and the extra flaps made it awkward to use. So we went back to the workshop and created the new and improved biFold clipboard.

    3. The Forecast

    This is where our team took a change in direction and decided to try something other than clipboards. We revisited the problem space (our office) and came up with the idea to plant something in the office; something that the HMs could interact with together as a team.

    4. The Queue Board #1

    We really liked the idea that the board made it possible for everyone in the room to see what was going on so we decided to go deeper with this idea and came up with the queue board. It was much more interactive than our forecast board and larger for visablity.

    5. The Queue board #2

    We wanted to keep the general layout and system of the queue board 1 because we got a lot of positive feedback from our coworkers and boss. We did make some slight modifications based on the inputs of our boss because he brought up a lot of good points we didnt immediately see.



    Testing




    Where is it now?




    Our last prototype (queue board 2) is actually in our office ever since doing a live field test. Most of our coworkers seem to interact with it pretty well and our bosses interact with it daily as well. We are currently in the process of ordering a new whiteboard to finalize the product but as of now it works in this prototype stage.
