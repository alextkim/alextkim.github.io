About this Project

Campus can be scary at night when waling alone. Talking to fellow students on campus, we found out that a surprising amount of students feel the need to use some sort of personal protection when walking around at night (eg. flash lights or pepper spray). This is ridiculous because our college campus should feel like a home away from home and the idea that we “need” self protection to simply stroll across campus symbolizes that we have an ever present fear in the back of our mind. We feel that safety should not be the sole responsibility of the individual but a community effort.

StoryBoarding

some of the ideas we came up as a group

Making storyboards of various ideas helped us envision what acting out our ideas would be like. For instance, as we kept delving into the reactions to the video, we realized that the reactions we were hypothesizing were not the reactions we necessarily wanted, and further considering the time and effort that would have to be put into making the video, the idea quickly depreciated as a potential design intervention. The other ideas were slightly easier to implement, such as a dissemination of pepper spray containers with a little blurb about our values, a booth to get items for personal safety, an email that would be seemingly sent out by UCSD, and a way to provide on-campus rides for people alone at night.



Prototypes

For our first prototype we wanted to focus on the fact that campus is empty

ProtoType #1 Night-life Map

Design Goal: Wanted students to realize a night community will make people feel safer at night, by providing a visual.

Various areas of campus (colleges, central parts of campus, etc.) would hold particular night-time events at time intervals throughout the year. Increased attention to these events and encouragement of being safe in groups at night would gradually encourage a night-life on campus. Students would also be encouraged to explore the campus by having to go to different areas of interest, which can cultivate familiarity with the campus. This critical design works to make safety a part of campus culture, where students are safe without having to physically own something or train themselves to think a particular way, because the campus environment enforces protection via a collective ideology.

ProtoType #2 1st Person POV: Video of Walking at Night

This is a first person POV video, separated by gender (male and female only at this time), that displays the experience of walking to a friend’s place on campus late at night. This design is critical of the gendered differences in thought about walking home at night, although at this time, we want to show both parties as being worried to emphasize that personal safety regardless of gender should not be on the student alone.

Our intended audience is Passive Allies (parents, faculty, staff, apathetic students), and we want to use this critical design intervention to shift them into Active Allies (students, especially those who live on campus). In turn, formerly Passive Allies will be in a better position to sway Passive Opposition (donors, people involved with the Chancellor) if the intervention gains traction.

Final Intervention

Launch day!

Step 1. Talking about Campus Safety. The first step of our project was pretty straight-forward; talk to students on campus and ask them if they wanted to UCSD a safer place. On week 10, our team went out on UCSD's Library Walk to set up our posters and talk to students about how the current state of night safety is on campus and how we can make it better. Along with us chatting to students, we had an interactive poster that said "I Can Help UCSD's Campus be a Safer Place by...". We provided three pre-written responses that students could choose from or they had the option to write their own.


our 4 options to choose from

Step 2. Creating a Visual. The list of options that pledgers could choose from serves two purposes; one, to remind pledgers the basic things we can do to serve each other; and two, to act as a visual aid for passer-bys to see that there is a growing community of people who are willing to help each other.

participants would tack their chosen option on the board to create a visual for others.

wearing the glowing bracelets would literally make students be a "Light On Their Campus"!

Step 3. Creating a Community

The last step of our final intervention was to create a community of people who would look after one another. For our project we gave out glow sticks bracelets to participants who took the pledge in hopes that they would wear them later that night. Our vision was that students walking at night would feel safer if they saw the glowing lights at night because the bracelets represent a community of people who took a pledge to do something.
