Need Finding Stage

Our team decided to tackle the very broad topic of creativity and how it seems to a dying art. In a society where mottos like nike's "Just Do It" or the ever popular yolo seems to be part of our everyday culture, its no surprise that people look towards the easiest and fastest solutions in order to keep pace in this progressive lifestyle. But the quick and easy solutions that people scramble to execute are often times not the best. As designers, we know that these quick seemingly easy and quick fixes are the first to pop in to our minds but when actually jumping into production can lead to a mess. That is why we decided to create a product to help individuals push their ideas and weigh their options before "just doing it".

Target Audience

Although the above image exemplified the pathways for a novice and senior designer, we sought to make a product that can be useful for anybody who needs to tackle a problem. Whether they are:

    1. An Artist who needs inspiration
    2. An engineering team that needs a creative solution
    3. A writer with writers block
    4. Or a student org that is planning an event

Anyone can can benefit from taking the time to ideate!

Our affinity diagram made from our user interviews

What We Found

1. As students, people seem to be pressed for time.

2. Most people felt that they get the most creative when under pressure.

3. People felt that they would be motivated if they had peers with them.

thinking about how to make the flow of idea8 before implementing

Our Solution

From our user research and observations when implementing idea8 there were a few core values we decided to focus on: Our product had to be quick, have some sort of pressure, and allow for easy collaboration between peers. In particular, We focused a lot on emphasizing the power of collaboration since more people = more ideas and included more features for those that chose to collaborate and gave the option to collaborate remotely since scheduling could be difficult.
