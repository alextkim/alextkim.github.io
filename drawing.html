Why Illustration?

As a kid I was always fascinated with the "delight" that came with great imagery and as I embarked on my journey to become a better designer I began to realize the power of visual design. There was only one problem; I was approaching the end of my sophomore year in college and I had no technical skills or understanding on the field and realized that I would encounter visual design sometime on my journey.

This was a problem for 2 major reasons:

    1. As a designer, I believe that it is important to able to connect with a production team
    2. Being able to draw what's on my mind is a useful skill for communication.

What I learned

One of the promotional posters for my Church group.

Although Illustrator and Photoshop cost a hefty sum out of my own pocket, I think they were well worth the time and money. Not only did I develop a useful skill for my field of study but I found a great hobby where I can release a creative outlet and I was able to help put my local church with some of their graphical needs.


Tools That Helped Me

Lots of online tutorials and critique from friends! Before diving into my own work I did a ton of tutorials to get a grasp of all the technical aspects that went into graphic art, until eventually I felt ready to create my own work.

Doodling in class also helped!
